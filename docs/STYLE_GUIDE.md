# Style Guide 风格指南

Each epoch has a distinct tone. All prose (Markdown files) should match the tone of its epoch.

## Epoch Tones

### epoch:01 — 混沌 (Primordial Chaos)
- **Tone**: Mythic, solemn, archaic
- **Language**: Grand, declarative sentences. Minimal dialogue. Creation-myth register.
- **Imagery**: Void, formlessness, cosmic scale, birth and sacrifice
- **Avoid**: Modern vocabulary, casual tone, technical language

### epoch:02 — 太初 (The First Dawn)
- **Tone**: Elemental, raw, powerful
- **Language**: Sensory descriptions — fire, water, wind, earth. Primal forces.
- **Imagery**: Dawn, light breaking through, elemental clashes
- **Avoid**: Political language, social structures

### epoch:03 — 神荒 (Age of Wild Gods)
- **Tone**: Wild, spiritual, untamed
- **Language**: Animistic, reverent. Spirits have personality and caprice.
- **Imagery**: Forests, mountains, rivers as living beings. Unpredictable divine behavior.
- **Avoid**: Bureaucratic language, systematic thinking

### epoch:04 — 万国 (Rise of Civilizations)
- **Tone**: Epic, political, diverse
- **Language**: Historical chronicle style. Multiple perspectives. Diplomatic and martial vocabulary.
- **Imagery**: Cities, armies, trade routes, cultural exchange
- **Avoid**: Mythic register (gods are distant now), modern slang

### epoch:05 — 礼法 (Age of Order)
- **Tone**: Formal, bureaucratic, codified
- **Language**: Legal and philosophical register. Precise, structured prose.
- **Imagery**: Courts, scrolls, rituals, hierarchies
- **Avoid**: Chaos, wildness, informality

### epoch:06 — 金潮 (The Golden Tide)
- **Tone**: Commercial, satirical, hyper-modern
- **Language**: Marketing-speak, ironic, self-aware. Capitalism as metaphor.
- **Imagery**: Markets, contracts, advertisements, commodification of everything
- **Avoid**: Reverence, solemnity (everything is for sale)

### epoch:07 — 元寂 (The Meta Silence)
- **Tone**: Existential, meta-aware, philosophical
- **Language**: Academic, introspective. Characters discuss narrative structure.
- **Imagery**: Patterns, recursion, stories within stories, the boundary of fiction
- **Avoid**: Simple heroics, straightforward narratives (everything is questioned)
