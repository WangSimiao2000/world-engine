# Epoch Guide 纪元指南

## epoch:01 — 混沌 Hùn Dùn (Primordial Chaos)

**Theme**: 天地未分，万物未生。

The world begins as undifferentiated chaos. The first act of creation — Pangu splitting chaos — establishes the fundamental binary of heaven and earth. This epoch ends with Pangu's death and transformation into the physical world.

**Key constraints**:
- No civilizations in the modern sense (only proto-consciousness like 元灵族)
- No technology, no social structures
- Characters are cosmic-scale beings, not humans
- Events are singular, irreversible, world-defining

## epoch:02 — 太初 Tài Chū (The First Dawn)

**Theme**: 元素初生，天地成形。

The raw elements take shape. Fire, water, wind, earth emerge as distinct forces. The world gains physical form but remains wild and unstructured.

**Key constraints**:
- Elemental forces dominate
- No organized societies yet
- Characters are elemental spirits or primordial beings

## epoch:03 — 神荒 Shén Huāng (Age of Wild Gods)

**Theme**: 万灵争鸣，神灵横行。

Spirits and wild gods roam freely. The world is animistic — every mountain, river, and forest has a spirit. Gods are capricious and powerful, but not organized.

**Key constraints**:
- Gods intervene directly but chaotically
- No centralized divine authority
- Mortals begin to appear but are insignificant

## epoch:04 — 万国 Wàn Guó (Rise of Civilizations)

**Theme**: 人族崛起，万国林立。

Mortals organize into civilizations. Gods retreat. Political structures, trade, warfare, and cultural exchange define this era.

**Key constraints**:
- Gods are distant — minimal direct intervention
- Multiple civilizations coexist and compete
- Technology is pre-industrial (bronze/iron age equivalent)

## epoch:05 — 礼法 Lǐ Fǎ (Age of Order)

**Theme**: 秩序至上，礼法森严。

One dominant civilization codifies laws, rituals, and bureaucracy. Knowledge is systematized. The world becomes ordered but rigid.

**Key constraints**:
- Centralized authority
- Formal philosophical and legal systems
- Creativity is constrained by rules

## epoch:06 — 金潮 Jīn Cháo (The Golden Tide)

**Theme**: 万物皆可买卖。

Hyper-commercialization. Everything — including spiritual power, divine artifacts, and even narrative tropes — becomes commodified. This is the capitalism metaphor epoch.

**Key constraints**:
- Economic systems dominate all aspects of life
- Satire and irony are appropriate
- Sacred things from earlier epochs are now products

## epoch:07 — 元寂 Yuán Jì (The Meta Silence)

**Theme**: 世界发现自己是一个故事。

The most advanced civilization discovers that the world operates according to narrative laws, not physical laws. This triggers an existential crisis that leads to the world's collapse and reset.

**Key constraints**:
- Meta-awareness is central — characters discuss narrative structure
- The meta layer (`world/meta/`) is primarily associated with this epoch
- The `observer` in meta annotations should be a civilization from this epoch
- This epoch ends with collapse — no "happy ending"
